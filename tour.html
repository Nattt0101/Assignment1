BigBang


T.O.P was the first Big Bang member to enlist for his two-year service per South Korea's mandatory military service requirement in February 2017. The remaining four members continued group promotions without him, holding several concerts in various countries until December. In 2018, members G-Dragon, Taeyang and Daesung similarly began their military enlistment, thus putting group activities on a hiatus. The digital single "Flower Road" was released in March 2018 as a gift and a farewell from the group to its fans ahead of a lengthy hiatus, as the band members complete their mandatory military service. Big Bang, also known as BIGBANG, is a South Korean boy band formed by YG Entertainment that consists of members T.O.P, Taeyang, G-Dragon, and Daesung. The group is known for shaping the K-pop industry and have been named the “Kings of K-pop”. Big Bang was formed in the mid 2000’s and they rose to fame after the release of their debut studio album, “Bigbang Vol. 1” which peaked at number 3 on the Korean charts. Since then, they’ve released two more Korean albums, “Remember” and “Made” and they have released five Japanese albums over the years, four of which earned them RIAJ certified gold. Their album, “Made” peaked in the charts in Korean, Japan, UK and US. Big Bang has also released five EPs in Korean and three in Japanese. Some of their most popular singles include “Tonight”, “Fantastic Baby”, “Bang Bang Bang”, “Flower Road”, “Blue”, “Loser”, “Let’s Not Fall in Love” and many more. Big Bang holds a record of 18 number-one songs that have remained atop South Korean’s biggest online music service, Melon, for 51 weeks. The members of the group have been the first Korean artists to enter Forbes’ Celebrity 100 and the 30 under 30 list of influential musicians. Big Bang has won an MTV Europe Music Award for Best Worldwide Act, have won Artist of the Year three times at the Mnet Asian Music Awards, and have won a Melon Music Award.
